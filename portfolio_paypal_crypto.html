Why two-sided networks change the game

A merchant crypto product only works if both sides of the network participate. Merchant acceptance drives consumer demand, and consumer demand drives merchant acceptance. I've managed products in two-sided payment networks where this flywheel is the core growth mechanism. The product decisions that matter most aren't about blockchain infrastructure — they're about reducing merchant integration friction (6 weeks → 8 days), making settlement rails selectable (instant crypto-to-fiat vs hold-in-crypto), and proving the economics (62% lower costs) with data that a CFO can present to their board.

What most crypto PMs get wrong about merchant settlement

Most crypto PMs pitch merchants on "accepting Bitcoin." Merchants don't want Bitcoin volatility. They want stablecoin settlement — the speed and cost benefits of crypto with the price stability of the dollar. The product insight: don't ask merchants to hold crypto. Let them accept stablecoin payments and auto-convert to fiat instantly. The merchant experience is identical to card payments except faster and cheaper. The customer experience is paying with their stablecoin balance. Both sides get what they want.

AI fraud detection: 93% accuracy on crypto transactions

Built an AI-powered fraud detection engine specifically for crypto transactions — different risk signals than card fraud (wallet history, transaction velocity, cross-chain patterns, geography mismatch). Partnered with data scientists on model training using crypto-specific features. The model catches fraudulent transactions before settlement, protecting merchants while maintaining sub-second approval latency for legitimate transactions.

Philosophy

Three beliefs about merchant crypto.

These beliefs were shaped by building payment products, analyzing settlement economics, and watching merchants make decisions.

Merchants care about economics, not technology

No merchant has ever asked me "does this use Ethereum?" They ask: "How much does it cost?" and "When do I get paid?" Stablecoin settlement answers both: 62% lower cost, settlement in under 5 minutes. The product's job is to hide the blockchain and deliver the economics.

Integration friction kills adoption

A merchant who needs 6 weeks to integrate crypto payments won't bother. I reduced integration to 8 days by building APIs with sandbox environments, webhook configuration, and a developer portal. The fastest path from "interested" to "accepting crypto" is the product that wins.

AI accelerates the PM, not just the product

I use Claude for requirements and Perplexity for research not because it's trendy — but because crypto moves too fast for manual PM. Requirements drafted 60% faster means I ship more experiments. Research done in 3 hours instead of 2 days means I catch regulatory changes before they become problems.
